NUTRITION GUIDE

Fuel your transformation the right way

✅

EAT MORE OF

  • Whole foods (rice, roti, dal, sabzi)
  • Protein every meal (eggs, paneer, dal, chicken, fish)
  • Calcium-rich: milk, yogurt/dahi, paneer
  • Fruits and vegetables daily
  • Nuts, seeds, peanut butter
  • Water — 2.5 to 3 litres daily
❌

REDUCE / AVOID

  • Sugary drinks (Coke, packaged juice)
  • Excessive junk food / chips / fried snacks
  • Skipping meals — especially breakfast
  • Ultra-processed foods regularly
  • Excessive sugar and sweets
💡

KEY PRINCIPLES

  • Don't count calories — focus on food quality
  • A slight deficit is okay; starving is NOT
  • Protein at every meal = muscle growth + recovery
  • Eat enough to support height growth
  • Pre-workout: light snack 30–60 min before
  • Post-workout: eat within 1–2 hours
📏

HEIGHT & GROWTH

  • At 14 you are still growing — prioritize nutrition!
  • Calcium + Vitamin D = strong bones and height
  • Sleep is when growth hormone is released
  • Eat enough — severe dieting can slow growth
  • A small deficit won't harm height if you eat nutritiously
  • Protein supports muscle AND bone development

RECOVERY & SLEEP

Growth happens when you REST

😴

SLEEP (8–10 HRS)

Growth hormone is released during deep sleep. Poor sleep = poor gains + poor height growth. Aim for a consistent bedtime and wake time every day.

  • No screens 30 min before bed
  • Dark and cool room for better sleep
  • Same sleep/wake time daily
💧

HYDRATION

Muscles are ~75% water. Even mild dehydration reduces performance and recovery.

  • Drink 2.5–3 litres daily
  • Extra water on workout days
  • Don't wait to feel thirsty
🧘

STRETCHING

Do 5–10 minutes of stretching after every session. This improves flexibility, reduces soreness, and prevents injury.

  • Hip flexors (important for posture)
  • Hamstrings and quads
  • Chest and shoulder opener
  • Upper back / thoracic spine
📆

REST DAYS

1–2 rest days per week are not optional — they're when your muscles actually grow and repair. Light walks or stretching on rest days is fine.

  • Never skip rest days
  • Light activity is okay (walk, yoga)
  • Eat well even on rest days
🏃

WARM-UP EVERY SESSION

Always warm up for 5 minutes before any workout to prevent injury and improve performance.

  • Arm circles x20
  • Hip circles x10
  • Leg swings x10 each leg
  • Light jog in place 60 sec
  • Jumping jacks x30
⚠️

LISTEN TO YOUR BODY

Soreness = normal. Sharp pain = STOP. Learn the difference between productive discomfort and injury.

  • DOMS (muscle soreness) fades in 24–48h
  • Sharp joint pain = rest and consult a doctor
  • Take an extra rest day if needed
